What is the da Vinci Machine

The da Vinci surgical system equips surgeons with advanced tools for minimally invasive procedures, although the term "robotic" can be somewhat misleading. It's important to clarify that it's not robots performing the surgery but rather surgeons who control the da Vinci system through a console, guiding the instruments in real-time. These instruments replicate human hand movements but offer enhanced precision and a wider range of motion. Additionally, the system provides highly magnified, 3D high-definition views of the surgical area, enabling surgeons to work through small incisions.

The da Vinci system, found in all its models, comprises three vital components: the surgeon console, patient cart, and vision cart. The surgeon console grants surgeons control over the instruments and provides a high-definition 3D view of the patient's condition. The patient cart houses the camera and instruments, manipulated by the surgeon from the console, while the vision cart acts as a bridge between the surgeon console and patient cart, ensuring seamless coordination throughout the procedure.

History of the da Vinci Machine

In 1995, Intuitive Surgical, co-founded by F. Moll and R. Young, took an existing surgical telepresence system and transformed it into the MONA robotic surgical system. Fast forward to March 3, 1997, when Himpens and Cadière made history by conducting the first robot-assisted cholecystectomy using MONA. This pioneering system boasted seven degrees of freedom, provided force feedback, and delivered a 3D visual experience, truly immersing surgeons in their operations. By 1999, MONA had evolved to perform gastric band placements and Nissen fundoplications. A study comparing it to traditional laparoscopy confirmed the potential of robot-assisted telesurgery, despite longer operating times. Ongoing technical advancements culminated in the development of the da Vinci® prototype, which not only improved control modes but also enhanced the ergonomics for surgical procedures.

The Creators of the da Vinci Machine

Intuitive, a groundbreaking player in the field of robotic-assisted surgery and the creator of da Vinci surgical systems, was founded back in 1995. Today, the company remains dedicated to crafting and refining robotic systems that empower surgeons with the added benefits of technology, allowing them to extend their capabilities in the operating room.

Impact of the da Vinci Machine here in the Philippines

The da Vinci machine made its debut in the Philippines back in 2010 when Medical City first adopted this cutting-edge technology, followed by St. Luke's Medical Center Global City. Interestingly, since its introduction, there haven't been any reported cases of medical mishaps. However, it's important to note that reliable data is scarce due to the limited utilization of the da Vinci system in the Philippines.

Now, let's talk about the potential impact this technology could have in the Philippines. If it were to be adopted nationwide, it could revolutionize healthcare accessibility by offering non-invasive surgical options to a wider population. Procedures like Robotic-assisted Laparoscopic Prostatectomy, Robotic-assisted Myomectomy, and Robotic-assisted Hysterectomy would become more dependable, providing patients with much-needed assurance. Such a move would underscore the commitment of healthcare centers to deliver top-quality care, ultimately offering peace of mind to patients facing surgery.
